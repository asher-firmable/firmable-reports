03 — Recommended Actions

What to do next

Priority-ordered. Action 1 takes 15 minutes and unblocks the decision on whether to keep any Microsoft inboxes at all.

1
First
This week · 15 minutes
Run cross-provider placement tests in SmartLead
Manually configure a spam test using Google sending accounts against Office365 seed inboxes, and separately, Office365 sending accounts against Google seed inboxes. The current data only covers same-provider pairs — cross-provider performance is unknown.
Why this is first: If Google→Microsoft also lands in spam, the issue is in the email content and affects everyone. If only Microsoft→Microsoft fails, it's a Microsoft sender reputation problem specifically — and that's what we're killing. This test is the decision gate for Action 2. Until you have this data, don't assume the worst-case scenario.
2
Next
This week / next · Based on Action 1 results
Pause underperforming Microsoft inboxes, shift volume to Google
Immediately pause the worst Microsoft inboxes (those confirmed as low inbox rate in tests 1–3), and redistribute their daily send quota to Google inboxes. Don't delete — just pause, in case cross-provider tests show they still have value. If the path to 90 Google inboxes requires new accounts, provision and begin warmup now (2–3 weeks to reach full volume).
Current capacity: 72 Gmail × 15 emails/day = ~1,080 sends/day. Scaling to 90 Gmail inboxes adds ~270 more. Keep a small Microsoft reserve (10–15 inboxes) as a hedge — over-indexing on one provider creates single-point-of-failure risk if Google tightens filters.
3
Ongoing
Weekly · Automated script
Weekly inbox health check — flag inboxes below 1% reply rate
Run a script weekly that pulls reply counts per inbox from the master inbox API and estimated sent volume from email account data. Flag any inbox below 1% reply rate over a 30-day window as KILL or WATCH, and output a sorted table to review.
What to build: scripts/smartlead_inbox_health.py — reuses get_email_accounts() and get_inbox_replies() already in scripts/smartlead_client.py. Output: inbox email · provider · replies · sent (approx) · reply rate · status flag.
